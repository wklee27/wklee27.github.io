Introduction

The project

Zurich Malaysia wanted a campaign platform for Malaysians to share their COVID-19 stories — moments of resilience, reinvention, and humanity during an incredibly difficult period. The #IAmMore microsite needed to feel emotionally resonant and brand-consistent, while handling user-generated content safely at scale.

My Role

What I did

I was responsible for the brand translation, UI design, animation design, and front-end development of the microsite. I worked closely with a backend developer on the content moderation system, and with Zurich's brand team to ensure every design decision was consistent with their visual identity. I also designed the admin dashboard used by Zurich's content team to manage submissions.

Figma Laravel Adobe Illustrator
Challenges

What made it hard

User-generated content platforms face a fundamental tension: openness encourages participation, but safety requires control. Every friction point in the submission flow — every warning, every field, every moderation message — had the potential to suppress stories. We had to make the submission experience feel free and expressive, while building robust protection against inappropriate content behind the scenes.

Approach

How I did it

I started with a deep dive into Zurich's brand guidelines, working with their team to develop a digital interpretation that felt warm and human rather than corporate. I used their blue and gold palette in a way that evoked optimism and solidarity. The submission flow was reduced to 4 steps — inspiration, writing, optional photo, confirmation — with auto-save, progress indicators, and conversational microcopy replacing form-like language throughout. For moderation, I collaborated with the backend developer on a 3-layer system: automated keyword filtering, AI content scoring, and a human review queue. I also designed the moderation dashboard — clean, batch-action oriented — that made it possible for a small team to review hundreds of submissions efficiently.

Outcome

The result

The microsite attracted 340,000+ unique visitors and over 12,400 story submissions — exceeding Zurich's campaign targets. The moderation system maintained 98.2% accuracy, keeping the platform safe without dampening participation. The campaign was internally recognised as one of Zurich Malaysia's most successful digital brand moments.

Key Learnings

What this taught me

Designing a submission experience is fundamentally about removing friction while maintaining trust.

Every question I asked users to answer had to earn its place — any unnecessary step was a submission we'd never receive.

I also learned that a well-designed moderation interface is just as important as the public-facing one.

The team reviewing 200 submissions a day needed an efficient tool, and the quality of that tool directly affected the quality of what appeared on the site.
