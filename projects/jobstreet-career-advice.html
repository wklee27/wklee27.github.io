Introduction

The project

Jobstreet is one of Asia's leading job platforms. Their career advice content was comprehensive and well-written — but static. Users would land on an article, read it, and leave without taking any meaningful action. The brief was to transform that guidance into an interactive experience that helped job seekers identify where they were in their career and what to do next.

My Role

What I did

I was the sole designer and one of the developers on this project. I was responsible for the full concept development — defining the gamification mechanic, designing the visual system in Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop, and building the interactive experience in HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I also planned the user flow and decision tree architecture.

Adobe Illustrator Photoshop HTML CSS JavaScript
Challenges

What made it hard

Career advice is a sensitive topic. Users have real anxieties about where they are professionally, and a poorly designed experience could feel judgmental or dismissive. The challenge was to create something engaging and honest — that felt like genuine guidance rather than a personality quiz gimmick. We also had to design for completion: most interactive web content has catastrophic drop-off rates, and we needed users to reach the results screen to get any value.

Approach

How I did it

After auditing 200+ existing Jobstreet articles, I mapped 8 distinct career situations people commonly find themselves in. I explored three gamification models — linear quiz, card selection, and scenario branching — and tested preference with a small group. Card selection won clearly: it was more visual, less clinical, and felt less like a test. I designed 48 illustrated career scenario cards in Adobe Illustrator, each representing a relatable situation. The decision tree was built with 48 unique pathways leading to 8 personalised output screens. I then built the whole thing in vanilla HTML/CSS/JS — no frameworks — optimised for fast load and smooth animation.

Outcome

The result

The experience achieved 4.2× higher engagement than Jobstreet's standard article format. Average session time reached 12 minutes, and 35% of users who completed the experience clicked through to relevant job listings — a significant conversion uplift for the platform.

Key Learnings

What this taught me

Gamification works when it's in service of genuine value, not novelty.
