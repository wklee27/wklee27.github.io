Introduction

The project

The Hub OUG is a specialty café and coffee roaster based in Kuala Lumpur, offering one of the widest ranges of single-origin beans in Malaysia. They needed a full brand and e-commerce presence — a website that could do justice to the quality of their product while making it easy for customers to discover, learn about, and buy coffee online.

My Role

What I did

I handled this project solo — brand identity, packaging design, UX/UI design, and full WooCommerce development. I also art-directed the product photography shoot, styling 48 SKUs across multiple setups and post-processing all images in Photoshop.

Adobe Illustrator Photoshop WordPress WooCommerce
Challenges

What made it hard

Specialty coffee is a discerning market. Buyers want to understand origin, roast profile, and flavour notes before committing to a purchase — they're not just buying coffee, they're buying a story and an experience. Standard WooCommerce templates treat products like commodities. The challenge was to build a custom experience that communicated expertise and quality at every touchpoint, without making the buying process feel complicated.

Approach

How I did it

I began with customer interviews — speaking to 12 regular Hub OUG customers about how they decided what to buy. The key finding: origin story and roast profile were the primary decision factors, not price. This completely changed the product page hierarchy. I designed the product pages to lead with photography, origin narrative, and tasting notes — with price lower in the flow. The visual identity used a restrained palette of warm grey, cream, and dusty blue — referencing linen and ceramic. I built a custom WordPress theme from scratch (no page builder), integrated WooCommerce with custom fields for product data, and optimised for sub-2 second load times despite the image-heavy content.

Outcome

The result

Online revenue tripled within 6 months. The Bean Finder became the highest-converting page on the site. Cart abandonment dropped 28%, attributed to the streamlined checkout and the trust signals we built into every stage of the purchase journey.

Key Learnings

What this taught me

E-commerce design is conversion design — and conversion comes from trust.

Every micro-decision, from the quality of the photography to the clarity of the delivery information, contributes to whether someone finishes a purchase or abandons.

I also learned that building from scratch, rather than customising a template, gives you enormous design freedom — and that freedom is worth the extra development time when brand quality is the product's main differentiator.
